\section*{Appendix A. Complete Pairwise Collapse Enumeration}
\label{app:collapse}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix A. Complete Pairwise Collapse Enumeration}

All $\binom{6}{2} = 15$ unordered pairs among the six induced regimes
are classified below by failure mode under the admissibility criterion
of Section~\ref{sec:collapse_strategy}.

\subsection*{Identity Instability}

Five pairs cannot be collapsed without violating extension-invariant identity:

\begin{itemize}
\item OBL/NOR (incompatible persistence across delegation and amendment);
\item OBL/OCC (obligation-bearing continuity conflicts with occurrence closure
at temporal realization);
\item OCC/ENR (time-indexed individuation conflicts with enduring persistence);
\item OCC/NOR (temporal realization conflicts with normative persistence across repeal);
\item OCC/CTX (time-indexed individuation conflicts with scope persistence).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Category Error}

Six pairs cannot be collapsed without producing a category error
under the neutrality constraint:
\begin{itemize}
\item OBL/ENR (normative attribution becomes universally admissible or requires
blocking discriminator);
\item OBL/CTX (obligation-bearing and scoping admit incompatible relation profiles);
\item NOR/CTX (modification and scoping relations admit no unified profile);
\item NOR/ENR (normative grounding relations misclassify purely enduring referents);
\item CTX/ENR (scoping relations conflict with unscoped enduring persistence);
\item CTX/REC (scoping relations misclassify descriptive records as applicability contexts).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Hidden-regime Reintroduction}

Four pairs cannot be collapsed without reintroducing
the original distinction as a hidden regime:
\begin{itemize}
\item NOR/REC (normative grounding requires \emph{isNormative} discriminator
to protect descriptive records);
\item OBL/REC (normative attribution requires discriminator to exclude
descriptive instances);
\item ENR/REC (measurement targets and descriptive records require
\emph{isDescriptive} flag to distinguish them);
\item OCC/REC (provenance discriminator required to separate occurrence anchors
from reports).
\end{itemize}
