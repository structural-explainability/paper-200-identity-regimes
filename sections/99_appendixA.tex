\section*{Appendix A. Complete Pairwise Collapse Enumeration}
\label{app:collapse}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix A. Complete Pairwise Collapse Enumeration}

All $\binom{6}{2} = 15$ unordered pairs among the six regimes
are classified below by primary failure mode under the admissibility criterion
of Section~\ref{sec:collapse_strategy}.
Each pair appears exactly once in the enumeration.

\subsection*{Identity Instability}

Six pairs cannot be collapsed without violating extension-invariant identity.

\begin{itemize}
\item OBL/NOR (incompatible persistence criteria: obligation-bearing entities
persist across delegation and compliance, whereas normative structures
persist across enactment, amendment, and repeal);

\item OBL/OCC (incompatible persistence criteria: obligation-bearing entities
are enduring referents, whereas occurrences are individuated by temporal
realization and do not persist through modification);

\item OCC/ENR (incompatible persistence criteria: occurrences are
time-indexed realizations, whereas enduring referents persist
independently of temporal realization);

\item OCC/NOR (incompatible persistence criteria: occurrences are
individuated by realization events, whereas normative structures
persist across amendment and repeal);

\item OCC/CTX (incompatible persistence criteria: occurrences are
time-indexed realizations, whereas applicability contexts persist
as enduring scoping referents);

\item OCC/REC (incompatible persistence criteria: occurrences are
individuated by temporal realization, whereas descriptive records
persist as enduring referents whose identity is not determined by
any single realization event).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Category Error}

Six pairs cannot be collapsed without producing a category error
under the neutrality constraint.

\begin{itemize}
\item OBL/ENR (normative attribution relations appropriate to
obligation-bearing entities would become structurally admissible
for all enduring referents, misclassifying non-normative entities
as potential obligation-bearers);

\item OBL/CTX (obligation-bearing entities admit normative attribution
relations, whereas applicability contexts admit scoping relations,
yielding incompatible admissible-relation classes);

\item NOR/CTX (normative structures admit modification relations
such as enactment and amendment, whereas applicability contexts
admit scoping relations such as nesting and refinement);

\item NOR/ENR (normative grounding relations appropriate to normative
structures would misclassify purely enduring referents as sources
of authority);

\item CTX/ENR (scoping relations appropriate to applicability contexts
would misclassify ordinary enduring referents as scope-defining entities);

\item CTX/REC (scoping relations appropriate to applicability contexts
would misclassify descriptive records as scope-defining referents).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Hidden-regime Reintroduction}

Three pairs cannot be collapsed without reintroducing
the original distinction through hidden discriminators.

\begin{itemize}
\item NOR/REC (normative grounding relations would require an
\emph{isNormative} discriminator to prevent descriptive records
from functioning as normative authorities);

\item OBL/REC (normative attribution relations would require a
discriminator such as \emph{isObligationBearer} to prevent
descriptive records from being treated as obligation-bearing
entities, reintroducing the distinction at the extension layer);

\item ENR/REC (measurement targets and descriptive records would
require an \emph{isDescriptive} flag to distinguish descriptive
representations from the entities they describe).
\end{itemize}
